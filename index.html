Loaded Pulled Pork Nachoes

Sicko Mode - Travis Scott

 

 

Travis Scott’s “Sicko Mode” is famous for its unpredictable structure, shifting beats, tempos, and moods throughout the track—from Drake’s smooth, laid-back intro to Travis’s explosive, high-energy drop, and finally to a woozy, spacey outro. Loaded nachos capture this same sense of layered complexity: each bite reveals something new, from crispy chips and gooey melted cheese to zesty salsa, creamy guacamole, spicy jalapeños, tangy sour cream, and hearty beans. Both the song and the dish thrive on contrast and surprise, with different elements taking the spotlight at different moments, yet everything comes together into one satisfying, cohesive experience.

ingredients

  • 1 bag tortilla chips
  • 2 cups shredded cheese
  • 1 can black beans (rinsed and drained)
  • 1 cup cooked pulled pork
Toppings:
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup sliced jalepenos
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup corn kernels
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
For Serving:
  • sour cream
  • gaucamole
  • salsa

Instructions: 

  1. Preheat the oven to 190 degrees
  2. Layer the chips in a large baking sheet
  3. Add Topings: sprinkle beans and meat
  4. Add cheese: cover everything
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until cheese is melted
  6. Add fresh toppings: once out of the oven add tomatoes, jalepenos, onions and corn
  7. Garnish with cilantro
  8. Serve immediately with sides of gaucamole, salsa and sour cream

Peach Cobbler

"Peaches" - Justin Beiber Ft. Daniel Ceaser & Giveon

 

The emotional tone of Justin Bieber’s “Peaches” mirrors the comforting, intimate feeling of a warm peach cobbler. The song is smooth, affectionate, and easygoing, with a relaxed rhythm that invites listeners to sink into a moment of pleasure and connection. Similarly, peach cobbler is not a fussy or extravagant dessert; it’s homestyle, hearty, and best enjoyed slowly, often shared with friends or family. Both the song and the dish evoke a sense of warmth and contentment, creating a cozy, indulgent experience that appeals to the senses and the heart at the same time. In this way, the mellow, tender mood of “Peaches” perfectly complements the soft, sweet, and inviting nature of a freshly baked peach cobbler.

Ingredients: 

Filling:

  • 6–8 fresh peaches (about 4 cups), peeled and sliced
    (or use 2 cans of sliced peaches, drained)

  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar

  • 1/4 cup brown sugar

  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon

  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg

  • 1 tsp lemon juice

  • 2 tsp cornstarch (optional, for thicker filling)

Topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

  • 1 cup granulated sugar

  • 1 tsp baking powder

  • 1/2 tsp salt

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

  • 1/4 cup milk (or more if needed)

  • 1 tsp vanilla extrac

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    Grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.

  • Prepare the filling:

  • In a bowl, mix peaches, sugars, cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, and cornstarch.

    • Pour into the prepared baking dish.

  • Make the topping:

    • In another bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

    • Stir in melted butter, milk, and vanilla until smooth.

    • The batter should be thick but pourable. Add a little more milk if needed.

  • Assemble:

    • Spoon the topping over the peaches. It doesn’t need to cover completely; it will spread as it bakes.

  • Bake:

    • 35–40 minutes, or until topping is golden and peaches are bubbly.

    • Optional: broil for 1–2 minutes at the end for extra browning.

  • Serve warm, ideally with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
